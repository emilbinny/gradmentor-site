Emil Binny

Founder & Lead Advisor, GradMentor

GradMentor was born from a simple observation: most applicants to elite MBA and PhD programs are qualified — but very few know how to present that qualification compellingly. The difference between an admit and a ding is rarely about grades or test scores. It's about story, strategy, and positioning.

Emil brings first-hand knowledge of the admissions process from the applicant's side — having navigated top-tier MBA and PhD applications himself, earning an MBA in Finance & Strategy from Rice University (Jones School) and an MA in Applied Economics from Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), Delhi. He is also a NET JRF qualifier and a registered CFA candidate.

His advisory work draws on research experience across multiple institutions, a deep understanding of how admissions committees think, and a network of advisors and alumni at programs including Harvard, Wharton, Chicago Booth, and INSEAD. GradMentor's approach is rigorous, personalised, and results-oriented — built around the client's unique story, not a templated process.

Our Philosophy

Guide. Strategize. Succeed.

The GradMentor tagline is also our methodology. Every engagement flows through these three principles.

Guide

Deep, personal guidance — not automated templates

Every client relationship begins with listening. We invest time in understanding your background, your goals, and what makes you genuinely distinctive before we advise on a single application element.

Strategize

Evidence-based strategy, not generic advice

School selection, positioning, narrative architecture, and application timing — everything is deliberate. We approach each application as a strategic product designed for a specific audience: the admissions committee.

Succeed

Results-oriented, milestone-driven engagement

Success means the admit you want, from the program that fits your goals — ideally with funding. We don't disengage after essay submission. We're with you through interview prep, waitlists, and final decisions.
